Adventure Time...!!

Adventure sports offer thrilling experiences, combining excitement, physical challenge, and a connection with nature. Activities like biking, paragliding, surfing, and other adventure sports are not only exhilarating but also offer numerous health and environmental benefits.
Biking is one of the most accessible adventure activities, with its origins dating back to the early 19th century. It strengthens cardiovascular health, burns calories, and provides an eco-friendly alternative to cars. Similarly, paragliding offers the unique experience of flying like a bird, soaring thousands of feet above the ground.

BIKING


Biking is an incredible mode of transportation and exercise with a rich history and numerous benefits. The first bicycle, called the "draisine," was invented in 1817 by Karl von Drais in Germany. Unlike modern bikes, it didn’t have pedals and was propelled by pushing with the feet.
Today, there are over 1 billion bicycles worldwide, outnumbering cars and highlighting their popularity.

PARA GLIDING


Paragliding is an exhilarating adventure sport that combines the thrill of flight with breathtaking views. It originated in the 1980s, evolving from parachuting and hang gliding, and has since become a popular activity worldwide.
Paragliders can soar as high as 18,000 feet (5,500 meters) above sea level, depending on weather conditions and regulations.

SURFING


Surfing is one of the oldest sports in the world, with roots tracing back over 3,000 years to Polynesia, where it was an integral part of culture and rituals. Modern surfing became popular in Hawaii before spreading globally in the 20th century.
Surfboards were traditionally made from heavy woods like koa, but today, they are crafted from lightweight materials like fiberglass and foam.
